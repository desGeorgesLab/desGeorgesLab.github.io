--- layout: page subtitle: cryo-electron microscopy and novel image analysis techniques to explore the dynamics and allostery of large macromolecular complexes bigimg: /img/Capture.PNG use-site-title: false --- Welcome to des Georges lab at the Advanced Science Research Center in New York City. We are a team of young individuals with very diverse backgrounds, among which includes chemistry, biochemistry, and physics. We are proud to be pioneers of the resolution revolution. With the use of techniques such as cryo-em, structures of large macromolecules can be obtained at near-atomic resolution. Our lab boasts the largest structure ever deposited in the protein data bank (PDB). Additionally, we further research and exploration on a variety of projects, including obtaining macromolecules structures, applying machine learning to further energy flow understanding, and running methods of molecular dynamics.
